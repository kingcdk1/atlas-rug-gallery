Wool & Heritage

The quiet asset under your feet.

A well made hand knotted rug is one of the few objects in a home that gets more beautiful, and more valuable, with age. Properly cared for, it outlasts the sofa, the paint, and often the house itself.

The Fiber

Why wool still wins.

Wool is the original performance fiber. It resists stains, releases dirt, holds color for generations, and springs back from pressure. Before there were synthetic carpets, there was wool, and the villages that still weave with it today use flocks whose bloodlines go back hundreds of years.

The best antique rugs are spun from wool sheared in highland climates, where the cold winters produce a longer, more lustrous fiber. That fiber is washed in river water, spun by hand, dyed with plant roots and minerals, and knotted on looms one row at a time. A nine by twelve can take two weavers a full year.

The Craft

One row at a time.

Every knot is tied by hand. The weaver reads a pattern, selects a color from the wool at her side, ties the knot around two warp threads, and moves on to the next one. A fine city weave holds up to one million knots, sometimes more. A village piece, a few hundred thousand.

This is slow work, done by people who learned it from their mothers and grandmothers. It cannot be rushed, automated, or faked. When you run your hand across the pile, you feel the years it took to make.

The Asset

Why a good rug appreciates.

This is why a good rug appreciates. There is a finite supply of antique pieces, the craft is disappearing in much of the world, and the rugs being woven today will be the antiques of tomorrow. Collectors have understood this for a century. Buyers are catching on.

When you buy from us, you are not buying a floor covering. You are buying a piece that will travel with your family from house to house, room to room, generation to generation. That is the difference between furniture and heirloom.
